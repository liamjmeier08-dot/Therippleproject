What is Rhabdomyosarcoma? (Background + Basics)

Rhabdomyosarcoma (RMS) is a rare cancer that begins in cells that normally develop into skeletal (voluntary) muscle. Even though it’s linked to muscle-forming cells, RMS can develop in many areas of the body.

RMS is most commonly diagnosed in children and teens. Because it’s rare, awareness matters: learning the basics and supporting research can help more families get answers and care.

Where it can appear

Head/neck, urinary or reproductive areas, arms/legs, and the trunk.

Common signs

A growing lump/swelling, pain, or symptoms from pressure on nearby organs.

How it’s treated

Often with chemotherapy, surgery, and/or radiation depending on the case.

Our Project: Why We Started a GoFundMe

The Ripple Project began as a simple idea: if two students can take action, that action can spread. Kyle and Liam started a GoFundMe to support Wolfson Children’s Hospital and raise awareness for RMS.

Cancer has impacted both of our families. That personal connection made this cause meaningful — and it pushed us to turn concern into action. Our goal is to grow awareness, encourage donations, and remind people that small steps can create real change.

Our “why”

Family experiences with cancer + a desire to help others and be part of change.

Our “how”

Fundraising through GoFundMe, spreading awareness, and mobilizing our community.

How you can help

Ripple effect
